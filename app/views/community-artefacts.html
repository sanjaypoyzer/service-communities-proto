Browse service community artefacts

Use the list below to design your next service community workshop.

  • Proposal document

    The purpose of this document is to layout the objectives and purpose of the prospective community and who might benefit from it. This document is sent around to prospective teams, departments and enables you to get buy in early on - and also helps to set expectations.

    This document should be created by the community during discovery.

    See examples
    • Proposal document for Import-export

  • Terms of reference

    The purpose of this document is to provide an overview of the given community, outlining its purpose, membership, responsibility and aims. In addition, it proposes a Service Community Sponsor role.

    This document should be created by the community during discovery.

    See examples
    • Terms of reference for Start a Business
    • Terms of reference for Employ someone

  • Service landscape

    The service landscape has been the focus of all communities and has been crucial for aligning disparate departments, prioritising work, etc. The service landscape enables a community to understand what government offers to users within a given space and through which channel.

    This document should be created by the community during alpha.

    See examples
    • Service landscape for Start a Business
    • Service landscape for Import-export

  • Agenda

    This document defines the objectives and activities for a community workshop. The document should be used during all phases of running a community.

    See examples
    • Agenda for workshop #12 for Start a Business
    • Agenda for workshop #4 for Employ someone
    • Agenda for workshop #2 for Import-export
